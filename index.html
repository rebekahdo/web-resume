Fantasy Basketball Project: Personal Project
  • With the increasing popularity of fantasy sports, employing machine learning to determine a team’s likelihood of winning a game and predict a player’s fantasy point score can significantly enhance a user’s fantasy sports experience.
  • To predict the winning team, we will leverage features like the home team's home record and the away team's road record. This classification helps users make informed decisions while picking a team to win a game. To achieve the most accurate model, we will rapidly iterate through various models and features, identifying the ones with the most robust predictive power. We are currently employing the K-Nearest Neighbors algorithm, but we will also consider other models such as Support Vector Machines, Naïve Bayes, and logistic regression, assessing their effectiveness through feature addition or removal.
  • In fantasy sports, forecasting a player's fantasy point score holds paramount importance. We will quantify fantasy points using ESPN's standard fantasy points format, associating points with various basketball statistics, such as points scored and rebounds. Our prediction model for a player's fantasy point score incorporates features like the player's average fantasy points over their last 20 games (or less, depending on the total games played), and the average fantasy points per minute the player's opposing team has conceded to the player's position, which we multiply by the player's expected minutes for the upcoming game, calculated based on their average minutes played over the previous two games. While our current model is based on linear regression for its effectiveness and interpretability, we remain open to the potential benefits of utilizing a neural network.
  • Our dataset was acquired through web scraping from basketballreference.com, using BeautifulSoup from the BS4 Python library. We will preprocess the dataset for machine learning using Pandas, while for our machine learning models, we will leverage the SciKit-Learn library. Should the need for a neural network arise, we will consider employing Keras.
